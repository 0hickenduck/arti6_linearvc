Can a voice change live in six understandable controls?

ARTI-6 + LinearVC explores whether speaker differences can be expressed by gently reshaping six vocal-tract motion tracks, instead of hiding the whole story inside an opaque speaker code.

The idea

Speech, translated into anatomy-sized handles.

ARTI-6 takes in speech and estimates a compact picture of how the vocal tract is moving over time.

The adjusted tracks are turned back into audio, then compared with a baseline that only swaps the speaker embedding.

The six controls

Small enough to inspect. Physical enough to reason about.

LA

Lip opening

How open the lips are.

TT

Tongue tip

The front edge of the tongue.

TB

Tongue body

The larger middle shape of the tongue.

VL

Soft palate

The velum, which helps route airflow.

TR

Tongue root

The back of the tongue near the throat.

LX

Voice box

The laryngeal region behind pitch and voicing.

The experiment

The useful comparison is not “can we convert a voice?”

It is whether the explicit articulatory transform adds something beyond the target speaker embedding alone.
