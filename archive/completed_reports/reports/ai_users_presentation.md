## Slide 2: Objective
- **Objective:** To leverage AI's multimodal capabilities (Vision and HTML/Canvas generation) to transform raw data and text-heavy outputs into intuitive, highly readable visual formats.
- **Goals:**
  1. Automate the logging of daily expenses and convert them into easily shareable visual summaries.
  2. Transform instructional text (fitness, cooking) into visual guides.
  3. Upgrade reading comprehension by visualizing abstract concepts into architecture diagrams and formatted layouts.
- **Speaker Notes:** Mention the core philosophy: AI shouldn't just summarize; it should design the summary for the specific human end-user. For example, sending a Markdown table of grocery prices to parents isn't as effective as an AI-generated menu graphic.

## Slide 4: How did you work with AI? (The Process)
- **Use Case 1: The Visual Daily Tracker (Expenses & Menus)**
  - *Action:* Snap a photo of a grocery receipt.
  - *Process:* Auto-synced to PC. AI reads the receipt using OCR/Vision, logs data into a daily tracker, and generates a visual menu/price summary.
  - *Result:* Sent to parents in Japan, allowing them to intuitively understand local prices and daily diet without reading spreadsheets.
- **Use Case 2: Visualizing Instructions**
  - *Fitness:* AI converts text-based workout plans into visual charts (showing movements and sets) for quick at-a-glance gym use.
  - *Cooking:* Recipes are generated with visual cues for meat/ingredient ratios and steps rather than walls of text.
- **Use Case 3: Canvas for Reading Materials**
  - *Action:* Asking AI to explain complex reading materials.
  - *Process:* Instructing the AI to use Canvas to output HTML instead of Markdown.
  - *Result:* Outputs feature custom colors, structured typography, and generated architecture diagrams, making learning highly efficient.
- **Speaker Notes:** Walk through the receipt pipeline as the primary example, as it involves a complete automated loop. Highlight the Canvas feature as a major upgrade to the traditional "TL;DR" text summary.

---

## Slide 5: What’s your finding?
- **Finding 1: Visuals Reduce Cognitive Load.** Converting markdown to visual charts or HTML significantly reduces the time needed to grasp information (e.g., glancing at a workout chart vs. reading a list).
- **Finding 2: Empathy in AI Output.** By tailoring the output format (like a visual menu for parents), AI communication becomes more empathetic and accessible to non-technical users.
- **Finding 3: Automation is Key.** Combining standard cloud sync (Google Drive) with AI multimodality creates a zero-friction pipeline. The user only needs to take a photo; the AI handles the rest.
- **Speaker Notes:** Share that the most surprising finding was how much better standard reading materials became when forcing the AI to act as a frontend developer (using HTML/Canvas) rather than just a summarizer.
